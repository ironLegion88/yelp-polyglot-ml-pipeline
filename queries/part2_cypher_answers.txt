Query 1: PageRank on User-Business Projection
================================================================================

SPEARMAN CORRELATIONS:
- PageRank vs Review Count: 0.773
- PageRank vs Avg Star Rating: 0.102

DISCUSSION:
A high correlation with Review Count is expected, as more inbound edges (reviews) natively increase PageRank. However, businesses that rank significantly higher in PageRank than in standard Review Count do so because they are reviewed by 'Power Users' (users who themselves have high centrality/activity). A highly weighted edge from an active Elite user transfers more PageRank than a review from a one-off account.

Saturated markets (High Similarity) indicate high competition for the exact same customer pool. A new business entering these markets must steal customers rather than acquire new ones.
Fragmented markets (Low Similarity) indicate distinct audiences, meaning lower direct competition.

Query 4: Betweenness vs Degree Centrality (Bridge User Analysis)
================================================================================

CENTRALITY OVERLAP SIZE: 7 out of 20

METRIC COMPARISON:
Group 1: 'Bridge' Users (Top 20 Betweenness, Outside Top 20 Degree)
   - Mean Reviews: 1283.9
   - Distinct Cities: 15.2
   - Distinct Categories: 60.4

Group 2: High Degree Users (Top 20 Degree)
   - Mean Reviews: 1813.0
   - Distinct Cities: 12.8
   - Distinct Categories: 40.2

DISCUSSION:
Users with high betweenness but low degree act as 'Bridges' between isolated social clusters. As shown by the metrics above, these Bridge users often travel to more distinct cities and review more diverse categories than purely high-degree users, who tend to be local 'hubs' clustered in a single geographic area.
